Today there will be an anti-case "How not to invest money in stocks" with a yield that the author himself stated at -20%. Minus 20%, friends!

Since the comment was left if it is published by the author himself, then I consider it important to analyze it. Perhaps this analysis will save novice investors from mistakes.

1) the Wrong portfolio is a very competent formulation. It is extremely important to have the right portfolio and be so confident in it that you can ignore any problem volatility and declines in the stock market. There will always be volatility, but even in a downturn, you can keep high-quality companies alive, knowing that their prospects are real.

If you need help with developing a competent strategy, please contact me, and I will be happy to help you.

2) Despite the fact that the author is talking about rare sales the most important thing here is that there were sales at all! Moreover, the sale of profitable shares is mentioned. We will not talk about specific securities, but selling a stock just because it is in the black is a huge mistake. If the company is of high quality , it should grow in price. Its growth is not a reason for sales.

On the other hand, a fall is not a reason to buy if it is justified (for example, receiving losses or canceling dividends).

3) Attempts to" try " to deviate from the strategy are also not the best example of investing. If you have chosen a strategy , you should stick to it. But, of course, here it is important to know: why you bought each security (and this is the first point - you need a high-quality investment portfolio).

To run from one stock to another - to engage in speculation, and therefore lose money, as 95% of speculators.

4) the Market may continue to fall. And it can start a sharp growth - this is unpredictably. And Yes, top-UPS are important, but they are not necessary if your investment distance is long. About 4 years of refund is also an exaggeration (if the shares are bought reliably). I have already given an example in which an investor, even after the crisis of 2008-2009, recovered capital faster. But there was a global decline in the number of 50%!

5) Make conclusions for 15 months - it is not worth it. Although I myself publish client cases for 1-2 years, but this is due only to the period for which people actually actually invest money to the current moment. Let me remind you that I recommend investing in stocks for a period of at least 3 years to achieve a sufficient level confidences.

And as for the "positive" and "falling market", no matter what they write to me or say - I do not expect a crisis. Call the current volatility a fall can only be someone who has not seen the volatility of the end of 2018 (which was quite recently and was several times higher - there was a decline of 18-20% and even then I would not call it "collapse").

In 15 months, the regular S&P500 rose by 8% excluding dividends. Since the "drawdown" of the end of 2018, when it was necessary to actively buy more, the growth was 21%! According to Microsoft, the growth is almost 44% in these 15 months. Even for a modest AT&T growth of 9%! And let me remind you: we do not take into account dividends, which are very important.

I don't know, what should be the composition of the portfolio, how low should be the diversification and what should be a severe bias in favor of loss-making assets in order to get such a loss with such a dynamic of assets in the portfolio?

Therefore, I would like Alexander to study fundamental analysis more closely approach the formation of an investment portfolio and be more relaxed about volatility in such a short period of time.

And remember - this is NOT the way to invest. Because Yes, SO you can stay without pants, and forever be disappointed in the investment.

Good luck in the future investments!
