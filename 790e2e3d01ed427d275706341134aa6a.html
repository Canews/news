ðŸ”¥How do I get a 500 $ dividend and why do I need an AIRBAG?ðŸ’¼

Friends, I invest in stocks for the purpose of living on dividends. And I believe that even after achieving financial independence and full dividend coverage of expenses, you need to have airbag.

Why, you may ask? Why is it needed? After all, the airbag is used to buy shares in a crisis or to hold out in case of unexpected sharp expenses?

Exactly.

And you may need it if the dividends are paid cut back. Yes, I am convinced that a reasonable portfolio diversification and careful selection of securities protects the investor from a reduction in dividends. Let one or two companies reduce the dividends in your large portfolio with an emphasis on passive income! But the rest make up for it with growth. But, alas, even a first-class business sometimes carries risks losses. Warren Buffett made a big bet on Kraft Hines - and yet the company suffered losses and reduced its dividend.

In the crisis of 2008-2009, the s&P500 dividend was cut by an average of 20%, although there were and are many companies that have been paying and increasing dividends for more than half a century.

So why do I need an airbag? It is necessary so that if you receive a dividend of $ 500 per month, and then it is reduced, you can continue to conduct your life as if nothing had happened and wait for the growth and restoration of dividends.

I have already given an example with the significance of an airbag to achieve passive income goals. Let's give the same calculations for reducing the dividend - take the same 20%.

Let's assume that you have achieved your goal of passive income of$ 500 per month. These monthly dividends of$ 500 (although rather quarterly, which you need to distribute for 3 months) provide you with some level of security life that you need.

And then-BANG! "the whole market has collapsed and the dividends have collapsed. Instead of a $ 6,000 dividend per year or an average of$ 500 passive income dividends per month, you only get $ 4,800 or $ 400 per month.

The scenario is disastrous because I can't imagine a dividend drop in the future a high-quality, diversified portfolio by 20% at a time. In a portfolio of 50 companies, for example, 10 must cancel their dividends ALTOGETHER in order for such a drop to occur.

And let's say you have an airbag of $ 3000 - six months of expenses, as I recommend.

And what happens?

If after collapsing by 20%, the dividend will grow by only 7% per year, and recover only after 5 years, you will have enough of this $ 3000 airbag to not reduce your consumption level at all!

Even if the growth of dividends is only 3.5% per year - a very small value! - and the restoration will take 8 years, then you only $ 4,700 of air bags will be enough to keep the standard of living alive.

For reference, I note that the s&P500 dividend, which fell in 2009, exceeded the value from which it fell already in 2012!

Therefore, remember that even with a catastrophic fall in dividends and a small rate of their return, the company will be able to this airbag will support the passive income you need!

Good luck with your investment!
