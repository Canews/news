The Central Bank has maintained its key rate. What it gives a private investor.

The Central Bank has maintained its key rate. What it gives a private investor.

The Central Bank of the Russian Federation has maintained its key rate of 6%.

Before that, he lowered it 6 times in a row. The rate can be changed 8 times a year, that's how many times the Central Bank's Board meets.

Maintaining the rate is a necessary measure to maintain the economy, which has been severely shaken by the spread of the coronavirus epidemic, a sharp decline in oil prices and a projected increase in inflation.

At the moment of the announcement of the bet, everyone paid attention to the brooch Elmira Nabiullina in the form of a tumbler. Many they took it as a sign that everything was going to be very unstable, a swing. The tumbler can be swung in different directions. It's fragile. But you can't drop it. And someone regarded the brooch as patriotism.

Saving the bid is a temporary measure. The key rate applies to everyone, not just banks.

The key rate is the Central Bank's main instrument. It affects the interest rate at which other banks will receive loans from the Central Bank. And then we will apply for loans at what interest rate.

Lower interest rates - loans become more affordable and more readily accepted, there is a greater exchange of goods, the economy is getting better, and growth rates are increasing. Reducing the rate is beneficial to the stock market.

An increase in the rate means that it is not profitable to borrow money, people spend less, and the economy is developing at a slower pace. They raise the rate to maintain the ruble and reduce inflation. An increase in the key rate is profitable bond market.

America lowered its rate to almost 0.

Russia can't just lower its bid. A lot that depends on it. For example, the price of oil.

If oil is cheap, the ruble falls.

If the rate is lowered, the ruble will become even cheaper and inflation will go up.
Central Bank of Russia it tries to support the ruble and prevent inflation from growing.

The key bid affects:

- mortgage and loan rates

- Deposit rates

- bond yields and prices

- the economy as a whole
